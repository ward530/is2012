The Better Healthcare Strategy

Prioritizing people through CSR initiatives

Posted by Taylor Ward on April 15th, 2023

When companies rise above and beyond the economic pressures through lending a hand and strengthening the feeble knees of society and their employees, the benefits of an outward-looking unilateral approach by businesses in corporate social responsibility are manifest. In examining the gains brought about by a stronger emphasis of internal and external corporate responsibility in a healthcare setting, this issue of CSR has proven to be a matter that must be carefully addressed and strategically considered. A certain advisory group defined CSR as “a balanced approach to address economic, social, and environmental issues in a way that aims to benefit people, communities, and society” (Avramchuk & Carpion, 2017, p. 27).

The delicate nature of the industry, as lives are both lost and preserved, must be nurtured with mutual reciprocity between individuals and organizations; the strenuous and intense environment in preserving human life can most certainly be pacified when accompanied by respect and kindness towards both those in their organizations and in the community.

Shedding light on the better healthcare strategy - the strategy of finding meaningfulness and contentment in the workplace through corporate social responsibility - must become more prevalent and incorporated in procuring means for improved hospital and health clinic success.

Benefits of CSR

Motivation & Enouragement: Focusing on the stress-reducing initiatives, positive and encouraging experiences in the workplace, and general subjective health of employees will provide a return on investments that cannot be overlooked.

Social Insurance & Market Reputations: Made abundantly clear in the media, the beating hospitals take in weathering the storms brought about by damaged reparations could not not be more evident, whether it be from accidental patient deaths, money fraud, illegal operations, and more; reputations are everything to a hospital in this regard.

"Consultants in this firm willingly took pay cuts to participate in corporate social initiatives, and their post participation likelihood of staying at the firm was greater than that of nonparticipants."

Decreased Turnover Rates & Industry Exit: The nature of the business proves it to be one of the most meaningful and fulfilling careers, but sadly the rates of shortages in the ranks of nurses and other sectors are increasing. A solution to this can be found in corporate social initiatives.
